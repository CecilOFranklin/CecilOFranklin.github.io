## 1. Background

ZBLAN (ZrF4-BaF2-LaF3-AlF3-NaF) is a heavy-metal fluoride glass with theoretical optical losses far below those of conventional silica fiber. Terrestrial production suffers from microcrystallite formation driven by gravity-induced convection during the cooling phase.

## 2. Microgravity Advantage

In microgravity, the suppression of buoyancy-driven convection allows for more uniform cooling, yielding fibers with significantly reduced crystallization defects. Multiple ISS experiments have demonstrated measurable improvements in optical attenuation.

## 3. Open Questions

- What is the minimum gravity threshold below which crystallization defects are effectively suppressed?
- Can centrifugal or electromagnetic containment methods approximate microgravity conditions terrestrially?
- What is the realistic cost-per-meter for orbital production at scale?
